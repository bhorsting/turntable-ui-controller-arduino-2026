Turntable UI Controller — Pin Wiring Guide

Board: Arduino UNO (ATmega328P)  |  Firmware: src/main.cpp  |  Serial monitor: 115200 baud (state + input logging)

Safety: Do not connect motors, solenoids, or H-bridge loads directly to the UNO pins. Use suitable drivers/relays. Outputs below are logic signals (typically 5 V / GND).
All digital inputs use INPUT_PULLUP in firmware. Unless noted, “active” means the pin is pulled to the level shown when the condition is true.

1. Serial / reserved pins (UNO)

USB serial uses D0 (RX) and D1 (TX) only. This project does not assign any signal to D0 or D1, so the H-bridge and relay outputs cannot “take over” the serial pins in software.

If serial garbles when relays click: that is usually electrical, not wrong pins — relay coils pulling current from the same 5 V/GND as the UNO, or noise on long wires. Use a separate 5 V (or 12 V) supply for relay boards, common ground only, flyback diodes on coils, and keep relay wiring away from the USB cable.

3. Arm lift H-bridge (D7 + D8 only)

No separate enable pin (D6 unused). De-energised = both A and B off. With OUTPUT_SIGNALS_ACTIVE_LOW = true (typical relay boards), off = D7 HIGH + D8 HIGH. With OUTPUT_SIGNALS_ACTIVE_LOW = false, off = D7 LOW + D8 LOW. Never drive both direction relays on at once.
MotionD7 AD8 BNotes (active-low relay board)
Off (idle)HIGHHIGHBoth direction relays off
Lift UPLOWHIGHDirection A relay on
Lift DOWNHIGHLOWDirection B relay on

While moving, one of A/B is on, not both. Swap A/B at the driver only if physical “up” is reversed.

4. Panel LEDs (D10, D11, D13)

Typical wiring per LED:

  D10 / D11 / D13 ----[ 220 Ω ]----|>|---- GND
                                   LED (anode toward pin)

Recommended: 220 Ω (150–330 Ω for brightness). Cathode to GND. Pin HIGH = LED on (steady or flashing per firmware).

5. Input wiring details

5.1 Buttons (D2, D3, A0) — active LOW

  +5V (internal pull-up in MCU)
       |
      [ ]  switch
       |
  Dx or A0 --------+
                   |
                  GND
  • D2 Arm Lift: maintained contact (latching) — ON/OFF state held until pressed again.
  • D3 Reject: momentary — pulse on press.
  • A0 Record End: same as buttons if using a switch to GND; or open-collector sensor pulling LOW when active.

5.2 Arm In (D4) — active HIGH

  At rest (parked):     D4 ---- open or to GND     → reads LOW
  Off rest (playing):   D4 ---- to +5V            → reads HIGH

Example: microswitch on rest post — NO to +5 V, common to GND, wiper/sense to D4, or NC arrangement per your mechanics.

5.3 Lift down limit (D5) — active HIGH

  Not at down limit:    D5 low (open or GND)
  At down limit:        D5 ---- +5V              → reads HIGH

Firmware stops lowering immediately when this input becomes active.

8. Ground and power

  • Common GND between UNO, driver boards, relay modules, and sensor returns.
  • UNO powered via USB or barrel jack (7–12 V recommended on VIN).
  • High-current loads (motor, actuator, solenoid) use a separate supply; connect grounds only (do not power motors from the 5 V pin).

9. Serial debug (optional)

USB serial 115200 baud: logs state changes (state -> …) and debounced inputs (IN … true/false). No extra pins.
